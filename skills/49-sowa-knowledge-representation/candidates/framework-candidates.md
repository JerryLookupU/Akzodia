# Framework Candidates

## Candidate 1: Logic/Ontology/Computation Triad

- Evidence: preface section "Logic, ontology, and computation"; landing page and TOC.
- Method unit: every KR design must specify inferential form, domain content, and computable realization.
- Triple verification:
  - V1 cross-source: supported by preface and TOC.
  - V2 predictive utility: selects different designs for rules, ontologies, process models, and retrieval systems.
  - V3 non-obviousness: avoids single-formalism or vocabulary-only KR.
- Status: accepted into final skill.

## Candidate 2: Representation Selection By Reasoning Need

- Evidence: TOC chapter 3 compares frames, rules/data, OO systems, natural language semantics, and representation levels; appendices cover predicate calculus, conceptual graphs, KIF.
- Method unit: choose representation from required inference and explanation behavior.
- Triple verification:
  - V1 cross-source: supported by preface plus TOC and index entries for many representations.
  - V2 predictive utility: answers graph vs rules vs ontology vs process model choices.
  - V3 non-obviousness: focuses on semantic fit instead of technology preference.
- Status: accepted.

## Candidate 3: Process And Change As KR, Not Workflow Afterthought

- Evidence: TOC chapter 4 covers times, events, situations, concurrent processes, constraints, and change; index has Petri nets, situation calculus, preconditions, postconditions, persistence.
- Method unit: if the domain changes over time, represent events, histories, preconditions, effects, and invariants explicitly.
- Status: accepted.

## Candidate 4: Purpose/Context/Agent Scoping

- Evidence: TOC chapter 5; index entries for context, purpose, agents, modal reasoning, intentional contexts.
- Method unit: attach validity context, purpose, and agent authority to assertions/rules that can vary.
- Status: accepted.

## Candidate 5: Knowledge Soup Policy

- Evidence: TOC chapter 6; index entries for default logic, fuzzy logic, nonmonotonic logic, open/closed world, theory revision, vagueness.
- Method unit: classify vague, uncertain, random, ignorant, inconsistent, and defaulted material before inference.
- Status: accepted.

## Candidate 6: Index-Based Knowledge Engineering

- Evidence: local source includes a rich subject/name index; TOC chapter 7 emphasizes sharing, schemas, multiple paradigms, representation relations, language patterns, acquisition tools.
- Method unit: maintain indexes linking terms, concepts, rules, contexts, processes, evidence, translations, and explanations.
- Status: accepted with adaptation to modern agent systems.
