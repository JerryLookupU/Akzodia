# Candidate Counterexamples

## CE1. Metadata Without Meaning

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: meaningless data cannot acquire meaning by being tagged with meaningless metadata.
- Skill effect: require sign/object/agent/purpose mapping.

## CE2. Syntax-Only Web Categories

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: categories such as string, number, date, and truth are insufficient for world semantics.
- Skill effect: reject models that only describe representation format.

## CE3. Superficial Vocabulary Standardization

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: standard vocabularies can hide differences in meaning and make interoperability harder.
- Skill effect: require translation rules, context, units, and agent purposes.

## CE4. Feature-Only Lexical Semantics

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: monadic features cannot express relationships among multiple entities.
- Skill effect: require semantic hooks and relation structures.

## CE5. Closed Definitions For Primitives

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: basic primitives such as agent, purpose, causality, and top-level categories involve circularity and implicit axioms.
- Skill effect: record constraints and open assumptions.

## CE6. Universal Theory Search

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: no single theory is best for all questions; engineers need adequate purpose-bound theories.
- Skill effect: validate approximation against competency questions.

## CE7. Role As Mere Noun Or Predicate

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: mapping role nouns directly to unrelated dyadic predicates makes language-to-logic mapping unsystematic.
- Skill effect: model roles through role types, `Has`, and signatures.

## CE8. False Precision In Participant Roles

- Source: `source-note section`.
- Warning: ambiguous natural-language roles should not be specialized before evidence is available.
- Skill effect: use common supertypes such as `Product` or `Source`.
